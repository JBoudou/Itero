Why iterative voting ?

Most common voting systems, like majority and plurality, have been proved by scientists to have some important limitations. In particular, these voting systems encourage strategic voting: voters usually first guess which alternatives are likely to win and limit their vote to these possible winners. This may results in outcomes in contradiction with the sincere preferences of the voters.

Iterative voting proposes to mitigate the impact of strategic voting by voting multiple times. On the first rounds, voters can vote for their prefered alternative wihout the risk of loosing their vote, because they know they could change their vote later on. Then, voters may change their vote to an alternative with more chances to win. This decision is not based on a guess, as in non-iterative votes, but on the results of the previous rounds of vote.

Welcome to Itero

Itero is an online platform that allows you to easily create and participate in iterate votes. Polls in Itero are divided in rounds. At each round, all participants in a poll vote for an alternative. The winning atlernative is the one that received most vote on the last round of the poll.
